Project Idea

Idea Name: Vocal Reviewer

Overview

This project is pretty much a software or a service that will allow users to review their vocals thoroughly and be given feedback and advice on how to improve as a normal vocal teacher or coach would. Through this, this will allow anyone of any skill level of singing to practice and improve upon their voice anytime and anywhere they wish, provided they can access this of course. Additionally, aside from giving advice on vocals, how it does give advice will be based on the software’s features that will allow it too accurately tell what pitch, key, and tone the vocalist is giving upon singing.

Motivation

As the project’s purpose is to help improve people’s vocals of anyone in any skill level, this can prove to be useful for aspiring performers or to those who just want to improve their voice for whatever reason that they see fit. Most importantly, this is accessible to anyone, and pretty much follows the same structure as a normal person would take lessons from a music teacher, warmups, advice, and music theory, it happens in this service as it would normally with a teacher in real life. And while somewhat similar products are out there in the market (i.e., Masterclass), this one is different in the sense that the user is guided by the software itself.

Description

For the user to use the software, they require a device, preferably an PC with Windows or MacOS or an Apple or Android device with the software installed. Either way, both must have an Microphone input for the software to register and process the user’s voice for feedback.

Aside from the products main purpose, there is a multitude of features that aid in reaching its purpose, all the while helping users in improving and understanding more about vocals and music theory as well.

Vocal Warmup and Exercise Sim

Before performing or going any further using the other software’s features, one of the most important things that a vocalist needs to do is to warm up their voice. This is performed through various methods, whilst taking account into the user’s vocal skill and preferences in warming up. Warmups performed can range from lip trills to performing vocal scales and warmups, strengthening, and warming up the user’s voice, as it would when an athlete warms up through stretching their limbs as an athlete’s physique and vocalist’s voice involves muscles that allow them to perform their activities respectively related to them.

Vocal Performance and Review

After finishing the warmup sim, the user may proceed with performing songs of any kind as they wish. Upon choosing the song, the user will be greeted with a screen of the lyrics of the song they are going to perform, along with the duration of the song, the scale and timing of which they are performing during the song and the scale, as if they were doing karaoke, but with a twist that the user’s performance is taken note of and registered such as an actual vocal teacher would hearing their student perform. And as with a vocal teacher, its their job to give feedback and advice on improvement. After finishing the performance of the song of their choosing, the software then displays user’s results, allowing the user to replay their performance, as well the tone, volume, pitch and key the song is originally performed in against how the user performed in their cover of the song.

Aside from this, in any area that the software notices that the user is weak in during their performance, they will provide the user some advice and feedback in either for the whole song or in a particular verse, bridge or chorus of the song. In addition to this, the software will take note of it in their next session with the user, choosing warmups of a different kind or in a different key that is appropriate for the user for their voice and appropriate in their vocals that they need to improve upon.

Pitch, Tone and Key detection and correction

These appear through the user warming up, performing a song, or doing vocal exercises. How it detects and registers the user’s voice is very precise, indicating the pitch, tone, and key they are giving off in their voice. As for correction, that appears at an end of a exercise or performance, showing a graph of the song’s original key, pitch and tone against how the user performed in their cover of the song.

Session Duration

Everyone in any field has their limits, and as so with singing, the software reminds the user the ideal duration they should sing and when they should stop, as further singing and strain their voice, effectively damaging it for longer periods of singing.

Tools and Technologies

Any Music/Voice recording software such as Audacity, Garageband, Adobe Audition or any other open source would be required as it not only allows for recording capabilities for the user to perform, but it also have features that allow it to pitch sounds up to the appropriate key. Additionally, Python, or any other coding language would be required to build the UI and allow functions to be made for the user to give input for what features they may use in the software.

Skills Required

Coding experience is a must for this product, any experience and skill in any coding language is allowed as long as it is enough to create what is necessary to have the idea come into fruition. Next, experience with the music software used is required as it is how the software performs in receiving the users’ vocals and comparing it to the original song. Lastly, Music knowledge, preferably vocal knowledge from a professional singer is required as it would be needed to not only give feedback to the user for their vocals, but as well as performing warmups of any kind to aid in the user’s strength and knowledge with their vocals.

Outcome

Should the idea come into fruition, the project outcome would result into a service of some sort. While it was never the intent to get rid of the traditional service of real-life music and singing lessons, at least this software is accessible to those who just wish to improve their singing for whatever purpose they wish to use it for. The accessibility of this service will bring many people in to give this a try, possibly creating a generation of vocalists out of this simple service, that will create a wave of artists and songs for the foreseeable future.
